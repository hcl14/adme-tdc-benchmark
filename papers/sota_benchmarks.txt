SOTA BENCHMARKS FOR TRAINABLE ADME-T METRICS
================================================
Sources: TDC leaderboards (tdcommons.ai), arxiv, biorxiv, openreview
Cross-validated against: Critical Assessment of TDC ADMET leaderboards (biorxiv 2026)
Date compiled: June 2026

IMPORTANT NOTE ON REPRODUCIBILITY
----------------------------------
A 2026 critical assessment (biorxiv:2026.02.26.708193) found that most top-ranked
TDC models had data leakage, unavailable code, or non-reproducible execution environments.
Only 3 methods passed ALL reproducibility checks:
  - CaliciBoost (Caco2 only)
  - MapLight
  - MapLight + GNN
MiniMol (listed #1 on several leaderboards) has documented data leakage issues.
Scores below list both "official TDC rank" and "verified SOTA" where they differ.

1. AQUEOUS SOLUBILITY (log S)
============================================================
Dataset:   Solubility_AqSolDB (TDC) — 9,982 molecules, regression
Metric:    MAE (log mol/L) — lower is better
Split:     scaffold-based

| Model             | MAE           | Status    |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------|
| MiniMol           | 0.741 ± 0.013 | LEAKAGE   |
| Chemprop-RDKit    | 0.761 ± 0.025 | VERIFIED  |
| DeepMol (AutoML)  | 0.775 ± 0.006 | unverified|
| AttentiveFP       | 0.776 ± 0.008 | unverified|
| MapLight + GNN    | 0.789 ± 0.003 | VERIFIED  |
| MapLight          | 0.792 ± 0.002 | VERIFIED  |
| Chemprop          | 0.829 ± 0.022 | VERIFIED  |

Verified SOTA:  Chemprop-RDKit   MAE = 0.761
                MapLight + GNN   MAE = 0.789

Key papers:
  - Chemprop: https://pubs.acs.org/doi/full/10.1021/acs.jcim.9b00237
  - MapLight:  https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00174
  - Survey:    https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16586

Cross-validation (ESOL dataset, 1,128 mol): XGBoost with 2D/3D Mordred,
  R² = 0.918, RMSE = 0.613 (arxiv:2307.05318). Delaney eq. RMSE ~0.85.

6. SKIN PERMEABILITY (log Kp)
============================================================
Dataset:   HuskinDB — ~585 compounds, regression (log Kp cm/s)
           No standardized TDC benchmark exists for this endpoint.
Metric:    R², RMSE — dataset-dependent

Best reported results (not on a unified leaderboard):
  - Fragment contribution model on HuskinDB:
      R²=0.50 (train, n=144), R²=0.40 (test, n=36), RMSE=0.96
      Paper: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10667307/
  - ML models (LGBM, XGBoost, GBM) on curated 441-record dataset:
      Best: LGBM/XGBoost outperform others; specific R² not extracted
      Paper: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10990209/
  - MD simulation (physics-based, not ML):
      validated against cryo-EM skin model
      Paper: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC9674988/

NOTE: Skin permeability is the weakest benchmark here — small datasets (~100-500
molecules), no standardized split, fragmented literature. HuskinDB is the best
open database. pkCSM (local paper) reported R²=0.683/0.801 on Alves dataset (51 mol).

Key database: https://huskindb.drug-design.de/data/
Systematic review update (2024): https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10881585/

7. CYP1A2 INHIBITOR
============================================================
Dataset:   Veith et al. via ChEMBL — ~12,000 molecules
           NOT included in TDC ADMET group (no standardized TDC leaderboard)
Metric:    AUC (AUROC)

Best reported:
  - DEEPCYPs (multi-task FP-GNN, 2023): AUC = 0.930 on held-out test (n=1,000)
    Training set: ~10,000 molecules
    Paper: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10123292/
  - Robust ML Framework (majority voting, 2023): AUC ~0.88-0.92 (isoform average)
    Paper: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10357106/

Cross-validation: Both studies agree CYP1A2 is the easiest isoform (highest AUC).
The multi-task approach consistently outperforms single-task models.

8. CYP2C19 INHIBITOR
============================================================
Dataset:   Veith et al. via ChEMBL — ~12,000 molecules
           NOT included in TDC ADMET group (no standardized TDC leaderboard)
Metric:    AUC (AUROC)

Best reported:

Cross-validation: Consistent with pkCSM paper (local) AUC=0.879 (2015, smaller dataset).
Note: CYP2C19 and CYP2C9 are structurally similar — multi-task models benefit most here.
